About Me

Throughout my career I have served over one hundred public high schools, thousands of underserved students and families, and dozens of high school and college faculty.

At The City University of New York (CUNY), I oversaw a college access, transition and success program serving high school and college students throughout NYC. I have since supported networks of New York City and Westchester county counselors in creating effective 9th through 12th grade college and career pathways. I have also worked within both the CUNY and SUNY systems on increasing college retention and graduation rates.

I received my Bachelor’s degree in English Literature from Binghamton University, and my Master’s degree in Social Policy from SUNY Empire State College.

The City University of New York

At CUNY, I led the launch and expansion of a program that served more than 2,000 high school seniors and 500 college students each year.

  • I facilitated professional development sessions and working groups with high school teachers, counselors and administrators
